## Abstract

This research paper establishes the mathematical foundations for 13 distinct visualization experiments within the MPN (Musical Psychometric Notation) Laboratory. We demonstrate that high-dimensional organizational states can be meaningfully projected into visual representations that preserve essential topological, dynamical, and spectral properties. Each visualization is designed as a **cognitive prosthesis**—an extension of human perception that makes the invisible patterns of organizational vulnerability visible, audible, and actionable.

The experiments span three categories:
1. **Conventional**: Direct mappings of layer structure and harmonic relationships
2. **Radical I (Topology & Dynamics)**: Phase space, knot theory, and nonlinear dynamics
3. **Radical II (Neural Physics)**: Graph neural networks, epidemiology, and percolation theory

## 2. Conventional Visualizations (Experiments 1-4)

### 2.1 Seven-Band Waveform (Exp. 1)
**Source**: RSCH-41 (Seven-Staff Fugue)

Each architectural layer $L_i$ generates a sinusoidal waveform:

$$
W_i(t) = A_i \cdot \sin(2\pi f_i t + \phi_i) + \epsilon_i(t)
$$

Where:
- $A_i$: Amplitude ∝ layer activity level
- $f_i$: Frequency ∝ layer's characteristic timescale
- $\phi_i$: Phase offset from inter-layer coupling
- $\epsilon_i(t)$: Noise term ∝ $\eta$

**Trauma Response**: Higher $\tau$ increases amplitude variability and introduces beat frequencies (dissonance).

### 2.2 Tonnetz Grid (Exp. 2)
**Source**: RSCH-39 (Musical Psychometric Notation)

The Neo-Riemannian Tonnetz is visualized on a torus surface $\mathbb{T}^2$, where:
- Horizontal axis: Circle of fifths ($P_5$)
- Vertical axis: Major/minor third relationships ($P_3$)

Chord transformations follow the PLR group:
- **P** (Parallel): $C \text{ major} \leftrightarrow C \text{ minor}$
- **L** (Leading-tone): $C \text{ major} \leftrightarrow E \text{ minor}$
- **R** (Relative): $C \text{ major} \leftrightarrow A \text{ minor}$

**Trauma Response**: Rapid oscillation between distant regions (e.g., tritone jumps).

### 2.3 Persistence Barcode (Exp. 3)
**Source**: RSCH-41 (Persistent Homology)

Topological features are tracked using the birth-death pair $(b_i, d_i)$:

$$
\text{Persistence}_i = d_i - b_i
$$

Long bars indicate stable topological features (robust organizational structures); short bars indicate transient instabilities.

**Trauma Response**: Birth rate increases, death rate accelerates → many short-lived bars (structural fragility).

### 2.4 7D State Evolution (Exp. 4)
**Source**: RSCH-40 (Orchestral Manifold Dynamics)

Seven concentric particle shells represent the 7-Layer Architecture:
- Shell radius ∝ layer activity
- Particle density ∝ entropy within layer
- Color ∝ layer identity (L0:gray → L6:purple)

**Trauma Response**: Shells distort, particles accelerate, cross-shell migrations increase.

## 3. Radical I: Topology & Dynamics (Experiments 5-8)

### 3.1 Borromean Knot (Exp. 5)
**Source**: Lacanian Psychoanalysis

The three Lacanian registers are represented as interlocked tori:
- **Real (R)**: Red ring — Zero-days, unprocessed trauma
- **Symbolic (S)**: Blue ring — Law, protocol, policy
- **Imaginary (I)**: Gold ring — Interface, trust, perception

The Borromean property: No two rings are linked, but all three are inseparable. Removing any one ring frees the other two.

**Trauma Response**:
$$
\text{Ring thickness}_i(\tau) = r_0 \cdot (1 - \tau \cdot \alpha_i)
$$

Where $\alpha_R > \alpha_I > \alpha_S$ (Real is most affected by trauma).

**Seldon Crisis**: When ring thickness approaches zero, the knot "unties" — complete organizational collapse.

### 3.2 Lorenz Attractor (Exp. 6)
**Source**: RSCH-14 (Ising Model / Stochastic Criticality)

The Lorenz system models deterministic chaos:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\dot{x} &= \sigma(y - x) \\
\dot{y} &= x(\rho - z) - y \\
\dot{z} &= xy - \beta z
\end{aligned}
$$

Parameter mappings:
- $\sigma$ (Prandtl number): Trauma responsiveness
- $\rho$ (Rayleigh number): System energy ∝ $\tau + \eta$
- $\beta$: Geometric coupling (fixed)

**Lyapunov Exponent**: $\lambda > 0$ indicates chaos (sensitive dependence on initial conditions).

**Trauma Response**: $\rho$ increases → system enters chaotic regime → trajectory fills both wings of attractor erratically.

### 3.3 Turing Patterns (Exp. 7)
**Source**: Morphogenesis / Reaction-Diffusion

The Gray-Scott model simulates pattern formation:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} &= D_u \nabla^2 u - uv^2 + F(1-u) \\
\frac{\partial v}{\partial t} &= D_v \nabla^2 v + uv^2 - (F+k)v
\end{aligned}
$$

Parameter mappings:
- $F$ (feed rate): $F = 0.055 - 0.03 \cdot \tau$
- $k$ (kill rate): $k = 0.062 + 0.003 \cdot \tau$

**Pattern Phases**:
- $\tau < 0.3$: Spots (isolated vulnerabilities)
- $0.3 < \tau < 0.6$: Stripes (linear attack corridors)
- $0.6 < \tau < 0.8$: Labyrinths (complex interdependencies)
- $\tau > 0.8$: Spirals (self-organizing chaos)

### 3.4 Neural Plasma (Exp. 8)
**Source**: Navier-Stokes Fluid Dynamics

Organizational "thought waves" are modeled as incompressible fluid:

$$
\frac{\partial \mathbf{u}}{\partial t} + (\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla) \mathbf{u} = -\frac{1}{\rho} \nabla p + \nu \nabla^2 \mathbf{u}
$$

Parameter mappings:
- $\nu$ (kinematic viscosity): $\nu = 0.001 + 0.01 \cdot (1 - \tau)$
- Heat sources: Trauma creates convection plumes

**Reynolds Number**:
$$
\text{Re} = \frac{UL}{\nu} \propto \tau
$$

- $\text{Re} < 1$: Laminar (smooth propagation)
- $1 < \text{Re} < 10$: Transitional (vortex formation)
- $\text{Re} > 10$: Turbulent (chaotic mixing)

## 4. Radical II: Neural Physics (Experiments 9-13)

### 4.1 Neural Propagation Network (Exp. 9)
**Source**: RSCH-03 (Gated Graph Neural Networks)

The GGNN message passing equation:

$$
\mathbf{h}_v^{(t+1)} = \text{GRU}\left( \mathbf{h}_v^{(t)}, \sum_{u \in \mathcal{N}(v)} \mathbf{W} \cdot \mathbf{h}_u^{(t)} \right)
$$

**Visualization**:
- Nodes: Organizational agents (color = DISC profile, size = OCEAN stability)
- Edges: Information channels (glow = weight)
- Activation waves: Trauma-triggered pulses propagating via message passing
- Dark Triad hubs: High-influence nodes that distort the force-directed layout

**Force-Directed Layout**:
$$
\mathbf{F}_{ij} = k_{\text{spring}} (\|\mathbf{r}_{ij}\| - r_0) \hat{\mathbf{r}}_{ij} - \frac{k_{\text{repel}}}{\|\mathbf{r}_{ij}\|^2} \hat{\mathbf{r}}_{ij}
$$

### 4.2 Epidemic Phase Portrait (Exp. 10)
**Source**: RSCH-11 (Epidemic R₀ Dynamics)

The SIS (Susceptible-Infected-Susceptible) model phase space:

$$
\frac{dI}{dt} = \beta S I - \gamma I = (\beta - \gamma) I \quad (\text{for } S \approx 1)
$$

**Basic Reproduction Number**:
$$
R_0 = \frac{\beta}{\gamma} = \lambda_1(A) \cdot \frac{\beta}{\gamma}
$$

Where $\lambda_1(A)$ is the spectral radius of the adjacency matrix.

**Phase Portrait**:
- X-axis: Infection rate $\rho = \beta$
- Y-axis: Recovery rate $\gamma$
- Critical separatrix: $R_0 = 1$ (the diagonal line)
- State particle: Current system position with trajectory trail

**Trauma Response**: Pushes state particle toward supercritical region ($R_0 > 1$).

### 4.3 Tensor Product Hypercube (Exp. 11)
**Source**: RSCH-37 (McKenney-Lacan Enhancement Analysis)

The 35-dimensional psychometric space is projected to a 4D tesseract:

$$
\mathbf{P} = (\mathbf{D} \otimes \mathbf{O}) \oplus \mathbf{T} \oplus \mathbf{B}
$$

**4D → 3D Projection** (Stereographic):
$$
(x', y', z') = \frac{d}{d - w} (x, y, z)
$$

With rotations in the XW, YW, ZW planes:
$$
\mathbf{R}_{XW}(\theta) = \begin{pmatrix} \cos\theta & 0 & 0 & -\sin\theta \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ \sin\theta & 0 & 0 & \cos\theta \end{pmatrix}
$$

**Trauma Response**: Warp factor $w \propto \tau$ distorts the projection (non-Euclidean deformation).

### 4.4 Spectral Convolution Waterfall (Exp. 12)
**Source**: RSCH-39 (Musical Psychometric Notation)

The musical score emerges from the convolution integral:

$$
M(t) = \sum_i \int_{-\infty}^{t} B_i(\tau) \cdot K(t - \tau) \, d\tau
$$

Where:
- $B_i(t)$: Bias channel $i$ signal
- $K(t)$: Convolution kernel (attack-decay envelope)

**Fourier Domain** (Convolution Theorem):
$$
\mathcal{F}[M(t)] = \mathcal{F}[\sum_i B_i] \cdot \mathcal{F}[K]
$$

**Visualization**:
- Spectrogram: Time-frequency representation
- Bias channels: Horizontal frequency bands
- Kernel window: Moving convolution mask
- Musical staff: Emergent note notation

### 4.5 Percolation Crisis Map (Exp. 13)
**Source**: RSCH-14 (Ising Model / Critical Phenomena)

Bond percolation on a 2D square lattice:

$$
P_\infty(p) = \begin{cases} 0 & p < p_c \\ (p - p_c)^\beta & p > p_c \end{cases}
$$

Where:
- $p$: Infection probability ∝ $\tau$
- $p_c \approx 0.5927$: Critical threshold (2D square lattice)
- $\beta \approx 5/36$: Critical exponent

**Spanning Cluster**: When the largest infected cluster connects left edge to right edge, the organization is fully compromised.

**Order Parameter**:
$$
P_\infty = \frac{|\text{Largest Cluster}|}{N}
$$

### 5.2 Performance Considerations
- **Canvas**: Limit grid size (e.g., 200×150 for Turing, 25×25 for Percolation)
- **WebGL**: Use instancedMesh for particle systems; limit draw calls
- **Frame Rate**: Target 30-60 FPS; reduce complexity on low-end devices

### 5.3 Accessibility
- Color schemes designed for colorblind users (avoid red-green only)
- Critical states indicated by multiple channels (color + shape + animation)
- Text labels provide quantitative interpretation
